Here are the 5 most amazing places you need to visit if you are traveling in Sri Lanka.

Try these!

  1. Sigiriya Rock Fortress.
  2. Galle Dutch Fort.
  3. Nine Arch Bridge.
  4. Yala National Park.
  5. Arugam Bay.

1. Sigiriya Rock Fortress.

Sigiriya or the Lion Rock is a World Heritage and one of the most stunning places to see in Sri Lanka.
Sigiriya was built by the fifth-century king Kashyapa I to survive from his brother Molligoda's attacks. During King Kashyapa's reign, Sigiriya was developed into a complex city and fortress. The fortress was the capital of the Sinhalese kingdom until Kashyapa was defeated.
Archaeologists of Sigiriya confirmed that there were many defense systems used to keep
Sigiriya secured from attacks. Some of those defense systems are Moats with crocodiles and brick walls.
Sigiriya is known for its dramatic rock face covered in elaborate paintings, which is one of its most unique characteristics. The "Sigiriya Frescoes" are ancient paintings of angelic women.
These frescoes have managed to maintain their brilliant colors over the years, showing the incredible skill and talent of the ancient craftsmen.
The panorama view from the surrounding countryside from the summit of Sigiriya gives
visitors an unforgettable experience and because of these things, Sigiriya is a great mark of
the Sri Lankan architectural brilliance and it keeps Sri Lanka on top of the list of most
beautiful countries to visit.

2. Galle Dutch Fort.

Galle fort was built first in 1588 by the Portuguese, then extensively fortified by the Dutch
during the 17th century from 1649 onwards. It is a historical, archaeological, and architectural heritage monument which increases the value of Sri Lankan history.
The National Maritime Museum is located at the Galle Dutch Fort, which serves as a showcase for Sri Lanka's nautical heritage and the forts' historical function as a center of trade.
The Galle Dutch Fort has, moreover, become a booming center for tourism,
culture, and the arts. The fort's streets are decorated with colorful street art, and its
courtyards frequently host cultural festivals, live music events, shops, and galleries providing a treasure store of locally produced handicrafts, jewelry, textiles, and modern art.
These things make Galle fort a very famous travel destination for both local and foreign
travelers.

3. Nine Arch Bridge.

The Nine Arches Bridge is a famous landmark located in Ella, Sri Lanka. The bridge was
constructed during the British colonial period in Ceylon (present-day Sri Lanka) in the early
20th century. It is part of the railway network built by the British to connect the hill country
with the coastal areas.
The surrounding landscapes and tea plantations create a breathtaking backdrop,
making Nine Arch bridge a favorite spot for photography and sightseeing.
Nine Arches Bridge is daily visited by hundreds of tourists, and It has become a must-visit
destination for tourists because of its unique beauty, historical significance, and impressive
architectural design.

4. Yala National Park.

Yala National Park is a huge area of forest, grassland, and lagoons bordering the Indian
Ocean, in southeast Sri Lanka. It's home to wildlife such as leopards, elephants, crocodiles, as well as hundreds of bird species. Yala National Park is the most visited and second-largest
national park in Sri Lanka.
Another main reason for Yala to be this famous is the safari rides. One of the most
sought-after safari experiences worldwide is available at Yala National Park. Visitors can
immerse themselves in Sri Lanka's natural beauty and get up close to the trees, plants, and
animals during the safari.
So because of these reasons, if you are a tourist, Yala National Park is one of the best places to visit.

5. Arugam Bay.

Arugam Bay is a small coastal town situated on the eastern coast of Sri Lanka, nestled along the pristine shores of the Indian Ocean. Arugam Bay beach is most famous for surfing and it
is one of the best beaches in Sri Lanka for all the beach lovers around the world.
Being near the Yala National Park, Arugam Bay is surrounded by loads of wild animals like
monkeys, elephants, and crocodiles.
There are many unique beach-side hotels and shops around the beach, and you can find some really unique artisan stores selling handmade clothing, cosmetics, and jewelry from there.
